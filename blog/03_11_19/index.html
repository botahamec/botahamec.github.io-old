Thoughts on the Citizenship Test

So I've been thinking about the following question: "Should there be a citizenship test for those who were born in America?" I kept thinking about this and I think I've come to a good conclusion. So here's my bold claim: I don't think there should be a citizenship test at all. That includes for immigrants.

I think a good place to start would be to explain why I don't think there should be a citizenship test for those who were born here. The common rationale for such a system is that it makes sense to make sure people know how the government works before they make decisions on it. The problem is that citizenship is also linked to other things, such as social security. It doesn't make much sense to deny someone social security based on whether or not they know the three branches of government.

"Why not just make a test in order to vote?" Well, some people would say that the Constitution forbids this and then just stop there. But that doesn't make for a very convincing argument. The reason why the Constitution forbids this is because states would require literacy tests in order to vote. Recently emancipated slaves did not know how to read and were therefore not able to vote. My basic point is that it's impossible to know that any given test is necessarily fair. I would say it's more acceptable to let uneducated people vote rather than exclude a large group of people.

So let's move on to immigrants. What exactly is the purpose of a citizenship test? If it's tomake sure they know how the government works, we already established why we can't use that. Now, it's going to seem like I'm backpedaling here, but trust me, I'm not. I'm going to make a bigger point later. I would make the argument that there should be a test for immigrants for one reason: to ensure that they know what the laws are so they know what is expected.

Here's the problem. Most immigrants are here long before they become citizens, whether it's by refugee status, green card, etc. We still benefit from them knowing the laws before they become citizens. So that's why I think the test should be administered as they come into the country. I think the best way of enacting this would be to give a pamphlet explaining what they would need to know for the test (and obviously this would be translated into every language) and make the test as fast as possible. And don't be stupid. They probably guessed that murder and theft are illegal. They also don't need to know about the laws regarding water safety, but make sure they know the non-obvious basics, like the rights regarding searches and self-protection.

Note that this would definitely not be the current citizenship test. Most citizens can't pass that. Also I would be in favor of making immigration easier, at the very least to compensate for the extra test. I don't feel qualified enough to explain what I think should be done on that front. I'm probably in over my head already.
